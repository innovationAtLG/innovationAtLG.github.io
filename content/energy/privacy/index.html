1. How We Collect Information

When you use any of the Services, we may collect information about you directly from you, automatically from your device, or from third parties.

Direct Collection. By using the Service, you may directly provide us with information. For example, you may click on buttons or enter text on the Services.

Automatic Collection. By using the Service, you may automatically provide us with information from your device(s).

Third Parties. We may also obtain information about you from our corporate affiliates and subsidiaries, third-party marketing, database, analytics partners, social media networks, or service providers.

 

2. The Information We Collect

As a general matter, we collect information that may help us in providing you with the current Services and provide you with future products and services.

Basic Identifiers. We may collect basic identifying information about who our customers or visitors are. For example, we may collect information about (a) the person (e.g., first name, last name), (b) contact details (e.g., e-mail address, phone number, postal address), (c) account details (e.g., username, password), (d) device details (e.g., device model name, log files, Internet Protocol (IP) address). Note that your address may be also be considered your geolocation. Basic Identifiers may be collected directly, automatically or from third parties when you sign in with a third-party account.

Network Activity. We may collect information about your Internet and other network activity. For example, we may collect (a) Log information (e.g., network type, network info) and (b) service usage information (e.g., how you interact with the Services). We may collect this information directly or automatically.

Geolocation Information. We may collect geolocation information about your device. We may collect this information automatically.

Personal Characteristics. We may collect characteristics about you (e.g., age, gender, marital status) from third parties so we can better understand who you are as a customer.

Inferences about Trends and Preferences. Through the course of your interactions with the Service, we may create or collect inferences about you for purposes of creating a profile to understand you better, such as your basic preferences, predispositions, behavior, attitudes, aptitudes, or other characteristics. We may collect this information directly, automatically, or via third party marketing, database, or analytics partners.
